Welcome to Fife

About Fife

Fife, also known as the "Kingdom of Fife," is a historic county in Scotland, situated between the Firth of Tay and the Firth of Forth. With its rich history dating back to Pictish times, Fife has played a crucial role in Scottish history and continues to be a vibrant part of modern Scotland.

Key Facts

• Population: Approximately 370,000
• County Town: Cupar
• Largest City: Dunfermline
• Famous For: Golf, universities, fishing villages, and coastal paths

Notable Locations

Fife is home to several significant locations, including:

  • St Andrews - Home to Scotland's oldest university and the "Home of Golf"
  • Dunfermline - Ancient capital of Scotland and burial place of Robert the Bruce
  • The East Neuk - A string of picturesque fishing villages
  • The Forth Bridges - Including the iconic Forth Rail Bridge, a UNESCO World Heritage site

Culture and Heritage

The region boasts a rich cultural heritage, from its medieval castles and ancient churches to its traditional fishing communities and mining history. Today, Fife is a dynamic region that combines its historic charm with modern development, making it a popular destination for tourists and a wonderful place to live.

Modern Fife

Today, Fife is known for its:

• World-class golf courses
• Higher education institutions
• Technology and renewable energy sectors
• Tourism and hospitality industry
• Rich agricultural lands
