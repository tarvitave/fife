Welcome to Fife

Known as the "Kingdom of Fife," this historic county in Scotland combines beautiful coastlines, medieval architecture, and rich cultural heritage.

About Fife

Fife is a historic county and council area of Scotland, situated between the Firth of Tay and the Firth of Forth. With a population of approximately 370,000 people, it's one of Scotland's most significant regions, both historically and culturally.

Key Attractions

Fife is home to numerous attractions, including:

  • St Andrews - Home to Scotland's oldest university and the famous golf courses
  • The East Neuk - A string of picturesque fishing villages
  • Dunfermline Abbey - The final resting place of Robert the Bruce
  • Falkland Palace - A Renaissance palace beloved by Mary, Queen of Scots

Natural Beauty

The region boasts 117 miles of stunning coastline, featuring the famous Fife Coastal Path. The landscape varies from sandy beaches to rugged cliffs, offering spectacular views across the North Sea.

Industry and Economy

While historically known for its coal mining, fishing, and textile industries, modern Fife has diversified into sectors including:

  • Tourism and hospitality
  • Higher education
  • Technology and renewable energy
  • Food and drink production

Did You Know?

Fife has been inhabited for over 8,000 years and was one of the major Pictish kingdoms, known as Fib, in the 7th century.
